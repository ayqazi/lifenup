# Short-Form Postnuptial Agreement (Working Draft for Solicitor Review)

**Important note:** This is a practical working draft informed by Rocket Lawyer UK’s embedded sample wording and general public guidance from UK solicitor sources on disclosure, fairness, needs, children, independent legal advice, and review.
**For greater legal weight, it should be reviewed by a family solicitor and each party should obtain independent legal advice before signing.**

## POSTNUPTIAL AGREEMENT

### BETWEEN

**Asfand Yar Qazi** of 33 Craighill Road, Leicester LE2 3FD
and

**Aisha Iftikhar** of 33 Craighill Road, Leicester LE2 3FD

(each a **Party** and together **the Parties**)

## BACKGROUND

1. The Parties were married on **3 November 2017** at **Redbridge Town Hall, Ilford**.

2. The Parties currently live together at **33 Craighill Road, Leicester LE2 3FD**.

3. The Parties have two children of the family:

   - **Aynie Qazi**, born **26/05/2015**
   - **Ayoub Qazi**, born **10/07/2019**
4. The Parties currently own the family home at **33 Craighill Road, Leicester LE2 3FD**, with an approximate value of **£420,000** and an approximate mortgage of **£290,000**, in joint names.

5. The Parties intend to complete the purchase of a replacement family home at **155 Jamie Marcus Way, Oadby, Leicester, LE2 4WB** on **29 June 2026** (the **Replacement Home**), for a purchase price of **£451,950**.

6. The Parties intend that this Agreement shall apply only from the completion date (the date on which the sale of the Current Home completes and the purchase of the Replacement Home completes, when the Parties move out of the Current Home and into the Replacement Home) (the **Move Date**). For the avoidance of doubt, this Agreement is not intended to regulate any period before the Move Date.

7. The Parties wish to record their mutual wishes and intentions regarding financial arrangements in the event of separation or permanent breakdown of the marriage after the Move Date, with the aim of reducing uncertainty, dispute, and litigation.

8. The Parties acknowledge that the welfare and needs of the children remain important and that the court retains jurisdiction in relation to children and financial matters as required by law. The Parties intend this Agreement to be persuasive and to carry as much weight as the law of England and Wales may permit.

11. The Parties further confirm that, as at the date of signing, neither Party declares any other material assets beyond those described in this Agreement and its schedules.

## PURPOSE AND PRINCIPLES

11. The Parties agree that this Agreement is intended to:
- protect identified separate property
- preserve certain gifts and inheritances from later claim where fair to do so
- recognise the housing and day-to-day needs of the children
- record the Parties’ intended approach to support and housing if the marriage breaks down
- reduce the risk of litigation and opportunistic conduct
12. The Parties further agree that this Agreement is based on what they presently believe to be a fair arrangement having regard to:
- the needs of the Parties
- the needs of the children
- the present disparity in income
- the importance of stable housing for the children
- the intention that Aisha should not be left without practical housing provision if separation occurs
- the intention that Asfand’s agreed separate property should remain his, and Aisha’s agreed separate property should remain hers

## DEFINITIONS

13. In this Agreement, unless the context requires otherwise:

**Agreement** means this deed and its schedules.

**Current Home** means 33 Craighill Road, Leicester LE2 3FD.

**Replacement Home** means 155 Jamie Marcus Way, Oadby, Leicester, LE2 4WB.

**Move Date** means the completion date of the sale of the Current Home and the purchase of the Replacement Home, when the Parties move out of the Current Home and into the Replacement Home.

**Permanent Breakdown** means separation followed by divorce proceedings, judicial separation, or such other clear and sustained breakdown of the marriage as makes reconciliation no longer realistic.

**Separate Property** means property intended by this Agreement to remain the separate property of one Party.

**Joint Property** means property not expressly identified as Separate Property.

## SEPARATE PROPERTY

14. The following shall be treated as **Asfand’s Separate Property**:
- his ISA, presently valued at approximately **£5,652**
- the sum of **£20,000** to be held after completion in a separate account as ring-fenced personal savings (the **Ring-Fenced Savings**)
- any property expressly agreed in writing after the date of this Agreement to be his separate property
15. The following shall be treated as **Aisha’s Separate Property**:
- her car, presently valued at approximately **£4,000**
- her gold, presently valued at approximately **£500**
- external inheritances and gifts received by her, whether before or after this Agreement, which are intended to be ring-fenced to her and/or for the children and not for Asfand
- any property expressly agreed in writing after the date of this Agreement to be her separate property
16. Past gifts made to Aisha by Asfand or by members of Asfand’s family are agreed to be treated as completed gifts and are **not to be reclaimed**.

17A. External inheritances and gifts received by **Asfand**, whether before or after this Agreement, are intended to be ring-fenced to him and not for Aisha, except that Asfand may, by a clear written instruction, irrevocably gift any such inheritance/gift (or part of it) to the children, after which Asfand retains no claim to that gifted portion.

17. The Parties acknowledge that a court may still consider needs, fairness, and the interests of the children, but they intend that the separate property identified above should, so far as lawful and fair, remain outside later division.

## JOINT PROPERTY AND FAMILY HOME

18. The Current Home is presently treated as joint property.

19. The Parties intend to purchase the Replacement Home at **155 Jamie Marcus Way, Oadby, Leicester, LE2 4WB**, with a purchase price of **£451,950**, completing on **29 June 2026**.

19A. The Parties intend that the Replacement Home will be owned in their joint names and that, unless later agreed in writing, the beneficial ownership is joint. The Parties do not intend to enter into a separate declaration of trust unless they later agree in writing and take legal advice.

19B. The Parties acknowledge that the intended lender is **Leeds Building Society**, under mortgage account number **73834929** dated **24 February 2026**, and that the mortgage is for **£373,450** over **24 years** on a **repayment (capital and interest)** basis. The mortgage is split into two parts: **£262,349** fixed at **1.79%** until **28 February 2027** and **£111,101** fixed at **4.30%** until **30 April 2028**, with the Society's Standard Variable Rate currently **7.74%** applying thereafter. The Parties record the current total monthly payment as **£1,737.29** until **28 February 2027**, **£2,578.53** from **1 March 2027** to **30 April 2028**, and **£2,789.80** from **1 May 2028** onwards. The deposit is intended to be funded from the proceeds of the previous house sale.

19C. A copy of the Leeds Building Society mortgage offer dated 24 February 2026 (account 73834929) is intended to be attached to this Agreement as supporting information.

20. In the event of Permanent Breakdown, the Parties’ main present housing intention is:

  - the children are to remain primarily living with **Aisha** in the family home, subject always to safety, welfare, affordability, and any court order
  - **Asfand may remain in the home for up to 6 months after he declares that he no longer seeks reconciliation, or until he has secured and moved into his own place, whichever comes first**, unless safety concerns or serious conflict require earlier departure
  - if Asfand starts any new relationship, he must move out immediately
  - the longer-term goal is for the Replacement Home mortgage (and title, if advised/required) to be transferred into **Aisha’s sole name**, when feasible
21. The Parties acknowledge that feasibility of transfer or remortgage will depend on lender criteria, affordability, and legal advice.

21A. The Parties agree that the joint ownership of the Replacement Home will continue until the home is sold or the mortgage is transferred to Aisha (and any related transfer of legal title is completed if advised/required).

22. Until remortgage, transfer, or sale, Asfand’s present intended support obligation is to pay **£3,500 per calendar month** (the **Monthly Household Contribution**) towards household costs, but only once he has officially moved into his own place.

23. Until Asfand has officially moved into his own place, he remains in charge of household finances and the Monthly Household Contribution does not start.

23A. The Monthly Household Contribution is a hard monthly amount. Any change to it must be negotiated and agreed by both Parties in writing.

23B. Reviews of the Monthly Household Contribution are flexible and may occur at any time if both Parties agree to have review discussions.

23C. The Monthly Household Contribution shall be paid by bank transfer to Aisha on the **last Thursday of every month**, starting when (a) separation is agreed upon by both Parties and (b) Asfand has officially moved into his own place.

23D. If Asfand remains in the home after separation for any period before he has officially moved into his own place, the Parties intend that he continues to meet household costs during that period.

23E. Late payments are intended to be treated as serious. Aisha will first give notice and a reasonable grace period before pursuing available legal remedies.

23F. For the purposes of this Agreement, “household costs” includes (non-exhaustively): mortgage payments (if any), buildings/contents insurance, council tax, gas/electric, water, broadband, TV licence, essential repairs/maintenance, groceries, children’s ordinary school-related costs, children’s ordinary clothing, reasonable local transport costs for the children, and ordinary child health/medicine costs.

24. Aisha intends to make repeated reasonable attempts to remortgage into her sole name when a lender affordability assessment permits, whether by full remortgage or by any mid-term transfer/removal of Asfand from an existing mortgage, and the Parties intend to cooperate with that process when feasible.

25. The Parties intend that the remortgage/occupation position should be reviewed if Aisha:

- remarries, or
- cohabits with a partner for a sustained period, with the occupation position otherwise governed by the 6-month grace period and immediate move-out trigger stated above

## INCOME DROP / AFFORDABILITY REVIEW

26. If Asfand’s gross income falls by **25% or more** from the current baseline of **£90,000 per annum** for a period of **3 months or more**, or he loses his job, this shall trigger a review of affordability.

27. On such review, the Parties intend:

- to act in good faith
- to review income, expenditure, mortgage obligations, and children’s needs
- to consider whether the support figure should be varied temporarily or permanently
- to consider sale only if the home becomes genuinely unaffordable after a reasonable cure period, including any reasonable attempt to negotiate parental contributions
28. The Parties do **not** presently intend a fixed sale date, and instead intend, where reasonably possible, to avoid sale while the home is needed for the children. If the home remains unaffordable even after reasonable attempts to vary contributions and negotiate parental support, sale may be necessary.

29. If a sale becomes necessary, the Parties intend the split of net sale proceeds to be **negotiable**, with a **50% / 50%** fallback if no agreement is reached.

29A. If Aisha demonstrates financial independence and is ready to have the Replacement Home transferred/remortgaged into her sole name, the Parties agree this triggers a negotiation of the Monthly Household Contribution, with the existing **£3,500** figure continuing until a new figure is agreed in writing.

## CHILDREN

30. The Parties agree that financial and practical provision for the children should be dealt with fairly and in accordance with law.

31. The Parties further agree that child arrangements are intended to remain flexible and are not fixed by this Agreement.

32. The Parties acknowledge that any future child arrangements may need to be resolved by agreement, mediation, arbitration, or family court if necessary, by reference to the children’s welfare at that time.

## WILLS AND DEATH

33. Neither Party currently has a will.

34. The Parties acknowledge that if inheritance intentions are to be reflected properly, each should make a will as soon as reasonably possible after taking advice.

35. This Agreement is not a substitute for a will.

## REVIEW OF AGREEMENT

36. The Parties intend this Agreement to be reviewed with the benefit of legal advice if any of the following occurs:
- acquisition of the Replacement Home
- significant change in mortgage obligations or housing arrangements
- a fall in Asfand’s income by 25% or more
- long-term illness or inability to work
- receipt of significant inheritance or gift
- remarriage or sustained cohabitation by either Party after separation
- any material change affecting the children’s needs
- any move outside England and Wales

## INDEPENDENT LEGAL ADVICE

37. The Parties acknowledge that:
- this Agreement should ideally be reviewed by a family solicitor
- each Party should ideally obtain separate independent legal advice before signing
- certificates or written confirmation of such advice should be retained with the signed document
38. The Parties further acknowledge that independent legal advice and full disclosure are important if this Agreement is later to be given greater weight.

## GENERAL

39. This Agreement is intended to record the Parties’ present wishes and intentions and to be persuasive to the maximum extent permitted by the law of England and Wales.

40. No variation of this Agreement is intended to be effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.

41. This Agreement shall be governed by the law of England and Wales.

# Clarifications Required

1. **Move Date definition**
   - Confirm whether “Move Date” is the day you first sleep there, the day keys are collected, or another objective date.

2. **Mortgage amount/term**
   - Mortgage amount and term for 155 Jamie Marcus Way are TBC; confirm once known.

3. **Mortgage offer attachment**
   - Confirm what exactly is “attached” (printed offer, PDF filename, or bundle reference) so the signed pack is consistent.

4. **Joint ownership / “beneficial shares”**
   - If you are joint legal owners with no declaration of trust, the default assumption is usually that beneficial ownership is joint too.
   - Confirm you want the document to state an intended default of 50/50 beneficial interest unless later agreed in writing.

5. **“Financial independence” test for Aisha**
   - Define the trigger precisely (e.g., lender agreement in principle for sole mortgage, affordability assessment, minimum income evidence, etc.).

6. **Support trigger vs. Asfand remaining in the home**
   - The postnup states the £3,500 transfer starts when separation is agreed and Asfand has moved out.
   - If Asfand stays in the home temporarily after separation, confirm the intended payment mechanics during that period.

7. **Payment timing edge cases**
   - If the “last Thursday” falls on a bank holiday or banking disruption, confirm whether payment moves to the prior working day.

8. **Late payment consequences**
   - You said “late payments are subject to legal proceedings” but did not specify the process. Decide whether you want:
     - a notice/grace period (e.g., 3 business days), and/or
     - interest, and/or
     - an express right to seek a consent order / CMS / court enforcement (solicitor to advise).

9. **Cohabitation/remarriage triggers**
   - The current cohabitation wording remains loose; define what counts (same address, nights/week, relationship status, etc.) and what it triggers (review, sale, contribution change).

10. **Sale “out of necessity”**
   - Define what “necessity” means (e.g., sustained arrears risk, lender action, insolvency risk, inability to refinance after X attempts).

11. **Ring-Fenced Savings mechanics**
   - Confirm whether the Ring-Fenced Savings are (a) retained from prior sale proceeds before purchase completion, or (b) held in an account after completion.

12. **What happens after transfer/remortgage to Aisha**
   - You want that event to trigger negotiation of contributions with intent to reduce them.
   - Confirm whether contributions continue at £3,500 until a new written figure is agreed, or stop automatically on transfer.
