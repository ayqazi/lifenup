# Parenting Plan (Phased and Reviewable)

**For implementation following separation**

## 1. Children covered

- **Aynie Qazi**, born **26/05/2015**
- **Ayoub Qazi**, born **10/07/2019**

## 2. General principles

1. The children's welfare is to come first.
2. Both parents are important in the children's lives.
3. The plan is intended to remain flexible unless and until a more detailed arrangement is agreed or ordered.
4. Neither parent should treat this plan as fixing final overnight arrangements.

## 3. Phased contact arrangement

### Phase 1 - Transitional period

While Asfand does not have accommodation suitable for the children to stay overnight:

1. The children will reside primarily with Aisha at the family home.
2. Asfand will have contact at the family home on the following schedule:
   - **Tuesday**: morning (help prepare children for school and school drop-off), afternoon (school pick-up and care until Aisha returns from work), evening (play and bedtime)
   - **Thursday**: afternoon/evening (play and bedtime)
   - **Saturday**: evening (family activity and Aynie's bedtime)
   - **Sunday**: afternoon/evening (play, Ayoub's bath, bedtime)
3. This schedule may be varied by mutual agreement.
4. The duration of Phase 1 is not fixed and will continue until Asfand has secured accommodation suitable for the children to stay overnight.
5. For the purposes of this plan, accommodation is considered suitable for overnight stays when:
   - each child has their own bedroom with adequate space to feel safe and comfortable;
   - the accommodation does not trigger the children's known allergies or food intolerances;
   - the accommodation meets basic safety and hygiene standards appropriate for children.

### Phase 2 - Established accommodation

Once Asfand has accommodation suitable for overnight stays:

1. The Parties will agree a revised schedule including overnight contact.
2. The schedule should aim to maintain Asfand's involvement while considering the children's welfare, school commitments, and practical arrangements.
3. If agreement cannot be reached, the matter may be resolved through mediation or the family court.

## 4. Health and welfare

1. The children have allergies/food intolerances.
2. Ongoing medicines and treatments are presently understood to cost about **£200 per calendar month** on average.
3. This cost is covered by the Monthly Household Contribution as defined in the Postnuptial Agreement.
4. Both parents should remain informed about:
   - diagnosis
   - medication
   - dietary restrictions
   - treatment changes
   - appointments where reasonably practicable

## 5. School holidays and special occasions

1. Arrangements for school holidays, birthdays, religious festivals, and other special occasions will be agreed between the Parties in advance, taking into account the children's wishes and both parents' availability.
2. The Parties intend to approach such arrangements flexibly and cooperatively, prioritizing the children's enjoyment and maintaining their relationships with both parents.

## 6. Education and day-to-day decisions

1. Both parents should be informed of major educational matters.
2. Both parents should be informed of significant health matters.
3. Ordinary day-to-day decisions can be made by the parent with care of the children at the relevant time.

## 7. Communication and future dispute resolution

1. The Parties intend to try to resolve future parenting disputes by discussion first.
2. If discussion fails, the Parties should consider mediation.
3. If necessary, unresolved issues may later be addressed by arbitration or the family court.
4. This plan is not intended to prejudice either Party's right to seek appropriate orders if needed.

## 8. Review

1. Either Party may request a review of this parenting plan at any time by giving reasonable notice.
2. Reviews should not be requested more frequently than **once per calendar month** unless there is a material change in circumstances.
3. Material changes include:
   - a change in accommodation (particularly Asfand securing suitable accommodation for overnight stays)
   - a change in employment or work patterns
   - a change in school arrangements
   - a change in the children's health or welfare needs
   - any safety concern
4. Any review must prioritize the children's welfare.

## 9. Relationship to Postnuptial Agreement

1. This Parenting Plan is a companion document to the Postnuptial Agreement dated [to be completed].
2. Financial matters, including the Monthly Household Contribution, are governed by the Postnuptial Agreement.
3. In the event of any conflict between this Parenting Plan and the Postnuptial Agreement, the Postnuptial Agreement shall prevail on financial matters, and this Parenting Plan shall prevail on day-to-day care arrangements.
4. Both documents should be read together.

## 10. Legal status

1. This is not a binding child arrangements order.
2. The court retains full jurisdiction over child arrangements and will decide any disputed arrangements by reference to the children's welfare at the relevant time.
3. This plan is intended to:
   - provide a practical working framework
   - reduce day-to-day uncertainty
   - show both parents' commitment to the children's welfare
   - provide a starting point for negotiation or mediation if needed
