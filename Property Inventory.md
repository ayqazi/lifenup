# Explanatory Page — Property Inventory: Purpose and Use

This inventory is designed to be signed by both Parties as a record of what is treated as:

- one person’s separate property
- the other person’s separate property
- joint property
- children’s property
- gifts not to be reclaimed
- sentimental / personal items

## Why this helps

A signed property inventory:

- creates a dated ownership record
- makes later false claims easier to challenge
- reduces dispute about who owns what
- helps preserve evidence of gifts and inherited items
- supports consistency between the inventory and the postnup

## Important limits

- It does not transfer title to land.
- It does not replace DVLA, bank, or Land Registry records.
- It is strongest as evidence, not as a title document.
- It should be updated if items are sold, replaced, gifted, or newly acquired.

## Best practice

- initial each page
- sign and date at the end
- add photographs where possible
- add receipts/serial numbers where available
- keep duplicate signed copies
