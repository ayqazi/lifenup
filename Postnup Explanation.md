# Explanatory Page — Scope, Limits, and How to Use These Documents

These documents are drafted for use in **England and Wales** based on the facts you supplied and on public guidance and sample wording from Rocket Lawyer UK and general solicitor guidance about postnuptial agreements, disclosure, fairness, needs, children, independent legal advice, and review.

## What these documents are for

1. **Short-form Postnuptial Agreement**
   Intended to record your shared financial intentions, identify separate property, and create evidential and practical friction against later opportunism.

2. **Signed Inventory of Property**
   Intended to record ownership, possession, and treatment of significant property and sentimental items.

3. **Parenting Plan**
   Intended only as a statement of present intentions and a framework for future discussion. It is **not** a final determination of child arrangements.

## What these documents are not

- They are **not a court order**.
- They are **not guaranteed to be binding**.
- The postnup is **not a substitute for independent legal advice**.
- The parenting plan does **not override the court’s duty** to decide child arrangements by reference to the children’s welfare at the relevant time.
- The property inventory does **not itself transfer title** to land, vehicles, bank accounts, or regulated assets.

## Why the postnup may still have value

Public UK guidance and template material consistently point to the same factors:

- full and frank financial disclosure
- fairness
- no pressure or duress
- independent legal advice for each spouse
- clear drafting
- review when circumstances change, especially children, illness, moves, or income shifts

A postnup that reflects those features is more likely to be taken seriously later than an informal note.

## Why solicitor review is still recommended

This draft is a practical working document, not a substitute for a family solicitor.
If you want greater legal weight, the **best next step is solicitor review**, ideally with:

- **independent legal advice for each party**
- certificates or written advice retained with the signed agreement
- review of fairness, needs, housing, and child-related provisions
- review of any housing-transfer/remortgage terms
- review after the 2026 house move

## Why the property inventory helps

A signed inventory creates friction because it:

- reduces ambiguity about ownership
- creates a contemporaneous record
- makes later false claims easier to challenge
- helps solicitors or a court see what was treated as separate, joint, gifted, or sentimental property
- reduces the scope for “I thought that was mine” behaviour

It is particularly helpful for:

- jewellery
- vehicles
- electronics
- furniture
- family gifts
- inherited items
- documents and personal effects
